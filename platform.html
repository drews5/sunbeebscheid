Our Vision For Carlson

A student government that listens early, acts fast, and stays close to the people it serves. We are relentless about follow-through.

Real
Connections

Real relationships over forced networking. We want leadership that stays close to the people it represents, making sure feedback actually leads to change.

  • Open up more ways to get honest feedback, instead of just checking a box.
  • Back student groups and campus partnerships that create genuine community.
  • Keep the door open so students always know exactly where to bring a concern.

Career
Success

We're pushing for better connections with Minneapolis employers and resources that students actually need. Professional development shouldn't feel scattered or anonymous.

  • Push for tighter partnerships with companies looking for Carlson talent.
  • Bring internships, panels, and recruiting tools into one central hub that's easy to use.
  • Focus on programming that opens doors, rather than just offering passive advice.

Belonging
at Carlson

Every student should feel at home here. We're building a culture where more people can see themselves in campus life without having to pretend.

  • Make sure different identities and backgrounds are recognized and supported.
  • Back the leaders already doing the ground-level work of welcoming people in.
  • Advocate for communication and traditions that feel human and inclusive.

Actionable
Initiatives

Concrete steps to improve the day-to-day student experience. We aren't just here to talk—we're here to deliver specific results for the undergraduate body.

  • Improve Business Board: Refactor board and team composition to include a dedicated Marketing Delegate to better represent all majors.
  • Maroon Lobby: Expand lobby hours and add more community games to make it a true student hub.
  • Caribou Coffee Chats: Official expansion of the Caribou chat series to build closer bridges between students and leadership.
  • Direct Advocacy: Establish a direct line for student groups and clubs to lobby for resources and space.
